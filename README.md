For this project, I built an Airfare Dashboard (preview in airfare.jpg) that brings airline pricing data to life with clear visuals and interactive insights. The dashboard highlights trends in average fares across different airport-pair markets, helps compare pricing behavior over time, and makes it easier to spot patterns that would otherwise be buried in raw tables. Behind it all is the Consumer Airfare Report: Table 1a – All U.S. Airport Pair Markets dataset, published by the U.S. Department of Transportation, which provides quarterly fare data aggregated across major U.S. airport pairs — not just cities. It gives a richer view of how average prices change across the domestic network. You can explore the original dataset yourself here: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/consumer-airfare-report-table-1a-all-u-s-airport-pair-markets
